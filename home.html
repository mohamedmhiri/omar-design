The African market is considered a considerable asset with great potential for exploitation; For example, sub-Saharan Africa will have the largest global growth in terms of import volumes over the next five years. Thus, it will have a strong wave of urbanization (from 472 million people in 2015 to 1.4 billion in 2050) and a growth of the middle class (a surety of 125 million people between 2015 and 2024) that change the landscape population of the region. Tunisia being part of this continent is likely to be able to take advantage of these transformations and to increase these exchanges with these neighbors: 3 of the 5 main products imported by sub-Saharan Africa are also included in the top 10 of Tunisian exports

This competition allows students to view the African market as a promising market in the future. It also allows them to show their creativity and share innovative ideas with a well-targeted audience (African experts). The student or the young entrepreneur or start-uper can through such competitions to confront the professional world and promote his ideas to investors on the continent.
